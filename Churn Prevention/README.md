**AI prompt design**

  - At high level:
    - Be specific about Objective: perform a root-cause diagnosis of GMV decline
    - Let the machine know your Role: Ecom Strategy Analyst
    - Construct a narrative of what happened and why, with clear phases and inflection points
    - Identify the primary cause of the decline and support it with data evidence
  - Key design:
    - Ground the model with a clear role: 'You are an expert-level E-commerce Strategy Analyst'
      - Decide model's tone and mindset
    - Set a precise objective: 'Your goal is to perform a root cause analysis of a GMV decline'
      - Be explicit about the business question you are trying to answer
    - Mandate evidence-based reasoning: 'Every conclusion MUST be followed by quantitative evidence from the data'
      - Force the model to tie every claim to actual metrics/evidence
    - Limit the scope: 'Choose ONE root cause from a predefined list of categories'
      - This avoids ambiguity and hallucination, also makes aggregation scalable across many sellers
      - Use 'UNKNOWN_INSUFFICIENT_DATA' if data is not sufficient to tell
    - Strict format: ensure consistency and scalability

--- English Analysis ---
### 1. Core Diagnostic Conclusion
The dramatic decline in GMV starting mid-July was not due to external factors, product issues, or creator channel failure. It was a direct and immediate consequence of a deliberate strategic decision by the seller to drastically reduce investment in two key growth levers: **product subsidies and advertising spend**. The business's high performance was synthetically generated by this spending, and its removal led to a predictable collapse back to a much lower baseline.

### 2. The Narrative of Decline: A Phased Analysis

The seller's 70-day performance can be understood in three distinct phases.

Phase 1: Investment-Fueled Growth (Late June to Early July)
The seller aggressively scaled investments to drive sales, successfully lifting GMV from a low base to significant levels.
*   Claim: The seller heavily increased spending on product subsidies, creator samples, and advertising to stimulate growth.
*   Data Evidence: Between June 24 and July 1, `prm_merchant_subsidy_product` increased from $61.74 to $2492.01, `ads_spending` rose from $48.42 to $173.85, and `daily_approved_sample` jumped from 41 to 119.

Phase 2: Peak Performance (approx. July 11 - July 14)
This period represents the peak return on the high investment, with GMV reaching its highest levels.
*   Claim: Sustained high investment resulted in a massive, multi-day sales peak.
*   Data Evidence: From July 11 to July 13, daily GMV (`usd_gmv_1d`) reached exceptional highs of **$4988.21**, **$6031.37**, and **$5858.57**. This was supported by peak daily product subsidies (`prm_merchant_subsidy_product`) of **$4537.57**, **$5460.35**, and **$5211.39**.
[Image]

Phase 3: Investment Cut & GMV Collapse (July 14-15 onwards)
The decline began abruptly when the seller withdrew the financial inputs that were fueling the sales volume.
*   Claim: The seller made a sharp, simultaneous cut to product subsidies and advertising, triggering an immediate GMV downturn.
*   Data Evidence: On **July 15**, `prm_merchant_subsidy_product` was slashed by 60% from $2854.10 (July 14) to **$1143.39**. Concurrently, `ads_spending` was cut by 58% from $199.48 (July 14) to **$84.01**. This directly caused `usd_gmv_1d` to fall 59% from $3152.99 to $1298.63 on the same day.

*   Claim: Following the initial cut, investment was kept at a new, much lower level, causing GMV to stabilize at a fraction of its peak performance.
*   Data Evidence: In the period from July 20 to August 16, `prm_merchant_subsidy_product` rarely exceeded $500 (averaging ~$250), and `ads_spending` rarely exceeded $100. Consequently, daily GMV (`usd_gmv_1d`) fell to an average of just a few hundred dollars.

### 3. "Tipping Point" Event Identification

The catalyst for the decline was a single-day, multi-faceted reduction in spending on **July 15, 2025**.

*   Data Before (July 13):
    *   `usd_gmv_1d`: $3,152.99
    *   `prm_merchant_subsidy_product`: $2,854.10
    *   `ads_spending`: $199.48

*   Data After (July 14-15):
    *   `usd_gmv_1d`: $1,298.63 (a -59% drop)
    *   `prm_merchant_subsidy_product`: $1,143.39 (a -60% drop)
    *   `ads_spending`: $84.01 (a -58% drop)

The near-perfect correlation in the percentage drop across GMV, subsidies, and ad spend on this specific date provides conclusive evidence of the cause-and-effect relationship.

### 4. Key Contributing Factors & Strategic Insights

*   High Dependency on Paid Inputs: The analysis reveals a business model where sales volume is almost entirely dependent on paid incentives rather than organic demand.
    *   Claim: The growth was "bought," not earned organically.
    *   Data Evidence: During the peak on July 12, the seller spent $5,460.35 on product subsidies and $270.52 on ads (totaling **$5,730.87**) to generate $6,031.37 in GMV. This indicates an extremely high, likely unsustainable, cost of sales.

*   Stable Product Strategy: The decline was not caused by changes to the core product offering.
    *   Claim: The top product and its pricing remained consistent throughout the peak and subsequent decline.
    *   Data Evidence: The `top1_prod_id` (1.729...e+18) and `top1_prod_price_tier` (5.0) were unchanged during the entire high-growth and decline period, ruling out a price hike or product change as the root cause.

*   Exclusion of Creator Seeding as a Cause: The seller did not reduce investment in providing samples to creators; in fact, they slightly increased it.
    *   Claim: The reduction in spending was specific to subsidies and ads, not creator sample seeding.
    *   Data Evidence: `daily_approved_sample` remained high at 120 on July 15 and even increased to 123 by July 20, demonstrating a continued commitment to this channel while other investments were cut.

### 5. Actionable Early Signals for Future Monitoring

To prevent unexpected collapses, the seller should monitor these leading indicators:

1.  Daily Merchant Product Subsidy (`prm_merchant_subsidy_product`): This is the most powerful leading indicator for this seller.
    *   Signal: Any single-day drop of over 30-40% in this metric should be treated as a red alert for an imminent GMV decline.
    *   Data Evidence: The 60% drop on July 15 was the primary catalyst for the collapse.

2.  Daily Ad Spend (`ads_spending`): This is a critical secondary indicator.
    *   Signal: A sharp decrease in ad spend, especially when combined with subsidy cuts, confirms a move away from growth-driving activities.
    *   Data Evidence: The 58% drop on July 15 occurred in lockstep with the subsidy cut and GMV fall.

3.  GMV-to-Investment Ratio (`usd_gmv_1d` / (`prm_merchant_subsidy_product` + `ads_spending`)): This efficiency metric is crucial for assessing sustainability.
    *   Signal: A ratio consistently near 1.0 indicates that growth is entirely dependent on spending and is likely unprofitable and unsustainable.
    *   Data Evidence: The ratio was approximately 1.05 on July 12 ($6031 GMV / $5731 Spend), highlighting the fragile, input-dependent nature of the sales peak.
